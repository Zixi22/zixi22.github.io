About Me

I am a PhD student at Institute of Psychology, Chinese Academy of Sciences, now visiting at Humboldt University of Berlin. My research focuses on social psychology, using the methods like computational text analysis, computational modeling, and psychometrics.

Research Interests

  • Inequality: How people form perceptions of economic and social inequality and how social inequality shapes psychological processes and behavior
  • Psychological resilience in unequal contexts: Situational and intrapersonal resources that buffer against the negative effects of inequality on well-being.
  • Technology and social stratification: How emerging technologies (e.g., AI) reshape inequality and influence intergroup relations.
